CHRONOLOGICAL HISTORY

Timeline

1996

Releases 'Infinite'

Eminem releases his debut album, 'Infinite', independently.

1999

Signs with Aftermath/Interscope

Eminem signs with Dr. Dre's Aftermath Entertainment and Interscope Records.

1999

Releases 'The Slim Shady LP'

Eminem releases his major label debut album, 'The Slim Shady LP', to critical and commercial success.

2000

Releases 'The Marshall Mathers LP'

Eminem releases 'The Marshall Mathers LP', which becomes the fastest-selling solo album in US history at the time.

2002

Stars in '8 Mile'

Eminem stars in the semi-autobiographical film '8 Mile'.

2003

Wins Academy Award

Eminem wins an Academy Award for Best Original Song for 'Lose Yourself' from '8 Mile'.

2005

Releases 'Curtain Call: The Hits'

Eminem releases his first greatest hits album, 'Curtain Call: The Hits'.

2010

Releases 'Recovery'

Eminem releases 'Recovery', a commercially successful and critically acclaimed album that marks a turning point in his career.

2013

Releases 'The Marshall Mathers LP 2'

Eminem releases 'The Marshall Mathers LP 2', a sequel to his earlier album.

2018

Releases 'Kamikaze'

Eminem releases 'Kamikaze', a surprise album responding to criticism of his previous work.

2020

Releases 'Music to Be Murdered By'

Eminem releases 'Music to Be Murdered By', an album inspired by Alfred Hitchcock.
